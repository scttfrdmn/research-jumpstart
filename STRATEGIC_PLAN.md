## Strategic Options Analysis

### Option A: Breadth Strategy
**Goal**: Show comprehensive catalog quickly

**Approach**: Create 10-15 Tier 3 starters across all domains

**Pros**:
- Rapid expansion of project catalog
- Shows full vision immediately
- Attracts diverse researcher audience
- Lower time investment per project (2-4 hours)
- Community can enhance to Tier 2/1

**Cons**:
- Many incomplete projects
- No deep examples in most domains
- Harder to prove concept works
- Risk of looking "shallow"

**Estimated Time**: 30-60 hours (15 projects √ó 2-4 hours)

---

### Option B: Depth Strategy
**Goal**: Prove the pattern works across domains

**Approach**: Complete 3-5 Tier 1 projects fully

**Pros**:
- Multiple complete examples
- Proves pattern is replicable
- Higher quality = better reputation
- Production-ready projects
- Strong portfolio for users

**Cons**:
- Slower catalog growth
- Higher time investment per project (4-5 days)
- Fewer domains covered initially
- Longer to show full vision

**Estimated Time**: 60-100 hours (5 projects √ó 12-20 hours)

## Risk Assessment

### Risks

1. **Scope Creep**
   - Risk: Trying to do too much
   - Mitigation: Stick to hybrid plan, resist adding domains
   - Impact: Medium

2. **Quality Dilution**
   - Risk: Too many projects, too little depth
   - Mitigation: Focus on Tier 2 before adding Tier 3
   - Impact: High

3. **Maintenance Burden**
   - Risk: Can't maintain 12 projects alone
   - Mitigation: Community contributions, clear ownership
   - Impact: High

4. **AWS Changes**
   - Risk: Services change, costs increase
   - Mitigation: Document patterns, not specifics
   - Impact: Medium

5. **Community Adoption**
   - Risk: No one contributes
   - Mitigation: Make it easy, clear value prop
   - Impact: Medium

### Mitigation Strategy

- **Focus on quality over quantity**
- **Finish what we start**
- **Clear documentation**
- **Make contributing easy**
- **Be responsive to community**
